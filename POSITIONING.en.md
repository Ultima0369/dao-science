# What Dao.Science Is: A Positioning Statement

## Dao.Science 是什么：一份定位声明

---

> Dao.Science is neither anti-science nor pseudo-science. It provides methodological support for science itself concerning the "first-person mind."
> It is not another science alongside physics or biology, but a **meta-tool** for all scientists: a science of "how to optimize the observer."

---

## I. Disciplinary Positioning

### 1.1 Dao.Science as a scientific methodology

The core flow of scientific methodology—hypothesis, testing, reproducibility, revisability—is fully preserved in Dao.Science, but its object expands from "the external world" to "the observer's own mind":

| Scientific step | Correspondence in Dao.Science |
|-----------------|------------------------------|
| **Hypothesis** | Mental content is 100% appearance of things; attention bandwidth is finite and trainable; neuroplasticity requires 3–5 years |
| **Testing** | First-person verification (self-empiricism) + third-person scientific evidence (neuroscience on meditation, hijacking, plasticity) |
| **Reproducibility** | Methods (two entries, four practices, four foundations of mindfulness, large/small-scale switching) are presented as an open-source toolkit; anyone can experiment on their own mind-body |
| **Revisability** | "Temporary certainty," "attending to the unknown," "this is not a definition"—always ready to revise in light of new evidence |

Dao.Science is not "another -ology"—it does not seek to establish itself beside physics, biology, or psychology. It is a **meta-methodology**: applying the spirit of scientific methodology to "the observer who does science."

### 1.2 What Dao.Science is not

To dispel common misunderstandings:

- **Not a religion**: It does not require belief in any supernatural entity. L0 (awareness itself) is first-personally verifiable, not something to be "believed."
- **Not mysticism**: All core propositions have mathematical formalizations (equations 1–21) and neuroscience literature support (100+ DOI citations).
- **Not "Eastern wisdom" cultural export**: Although the vocabulary comes from Daoist/Buddhist traditions, the argumentative structure is based on predictive coding, active inference, and cognitive neuroscience—contemporary scientific frameworks.
- **Not "science-wrapped spirituality"**: It offers no shortcuts, promises no "enlightenment," and sells no experiences. It only provides maps; the path must be walked by oneself.
- **Not anti-science**: It does not deny the validity of scientific method. On the contrary, it argues that scientific method needs to be extended to the dimension of "the observer itself."

### 1.3 A statement on "hard boundaries" and "soft connections"

Above the atomic nucleus, all things possess absolute difference. Every entity—defined by the boundary of its body—has a unique geometry, characteristics, and functions. A stone, a tree, a human being are not abstractly identical "things," but existences with irreducible physical specificity.

Other beings that interact with them—different people, for example—will have different cognitions, understandings, and value judgments about the same object. Divergence and consensus coexist.

**This reminds us: when we speak of objects, physics, and laws, we are actually speaking of the details of how the human mind enables them.** The mind is not a simple copy of a single objective "territory," but a "map" generated by each entity based on its own boundary, structure, and history.

Therefore, from the moment this project is open-sourced, it enters the scope of universal axiom testing—it uses language acceptable to current scientific paradigms and methodologies; yet at the same time, the project repeatedly reminds readers:

> **From a first-person perspective, verify through your own practice. The resulting cognition should strive to conform both to axioms and to the actual mind-body reality of your own living existence.**

All statements in this project have only **reference value** for readers, not the authority to **override another's will**. We do not dare to claim who is right or wrong. We provide only a map—a map of "how to optimize the observer." The path must be walked by each person on their own mind-body.

### 1.4 The principle of least action: a meta-design standard for the project

This project also has a hidden design standard: the **principle of least action**.

In physics, the path that actually occurs is the one that extremizes action. In Dao.Science, we translate this principle into a cognitive and organizational meta-standard:

> **A good concept, a good module, a good design should reduce the total cognitive action required to understand the whole project, not increase it.**

This means:
- Every new concept must serve an irreplaceable function;
- Every equation must eliminate ambiguity rather than create new confusion;
- Every application case must connect to the core framework, not float as an anecdote;
- Every warning box is not decoration but a real guardrail against project misuse.

Efficiency, elegance, rigorous and precise details—these are not aesthetic whims but system-level engineering requirements. When every part approaches its path of least action, the whole project will emerge with value far exceeding the sum of its parts: it will become a manual that **conforms to axioms, to mind-body reality, and produces practical utility at the level of survival needs**.

Phase transitions can flip a system from one state to another; only emergence allows a system to continuously grow new adaptive capacities in the face of rapidly changing environments.

## II. Core Propositions

Dao.Science rests on three testable core propositions:

### Proposition 1: Dao = the dynamic process of cognitive-action enabling

$$\text{Dao} \equiv -\nabla_\theta G(\pi_\theta)$$

where $\pi_\theta$ is a cognitive-action strategy parameterized by continuous parameters $\theta \in \mathbb{R}^d$. The discrete strategy-selection form is $\pi_{\text{accord}} = \arg\min_\pi G(\pi)$.

Dao is not truth, not law, not the source of all things—Dao is **what you are doing**. It is the enabling process of mind from L0 (awareness itself) through L4 (rational cooperation) to L7 (self-destruction). You say it is truth? Then live that truth. You say it is law? Then discover and verify that law.

### Proposition 2: One = unobstructed awareness bandwidth

$$AB(t) = 1 - \frac{R_{\text{DMN}}(t) - R_0}{R_{\text{max}} - R_0}$$

where $R_{\text{DMN}}(t)$ is the standardized change in DMN (e.g., PCC/mPFC) BOLD signal relative to resting baseline, $R_0$ is baseline, and $R_{\text{max}}$ is the maximum observed value; $AB(t) \in [0, 1]$ is the relative available proportion of awareness bandwidth.

"One" is not mystical "union of heaven and human." It is the maximum available bandwidth when cognitive resources are not blocked by self-referential processing (DMN). It can be directly measured by fMRI/EEG/behavioral experiments.

### Proposition 3: Mental content is 100% appearance of things, not the totality of things

All conscious experience—including the most "objective" scientific observation—is an internal model constructed by the brain as a prediction engine (appearance/map), not external reality itself (thing-in-itself/territory). This is not nihilism—appearances are real, useful, and testable—but they are not the whole.

## IV. Ethical Endpoint: Self-Benefit and Benefit to Others

The ultimate endpoint of Dao.Science is not "greater discovery," not "more advanced technology," not "a loftier state"—but:

**How an explorer can simultaneously live out scientific rigor, the philosopher's insight, the bodhisattva's compassion, and the flavorful everyday life of an ordinary person.**

This returns to the project's starting point: "first, keep your little life safe." A scientist who can flexibly zoom cognitive focus should first be someone who can settle their own body-mind, tend intimate relationships, and live daily life with joy and peace. Otherwise, however great the discovery, it is a tree without roots.

What Dao.Science ultimately explores is not the ultimate formula of the universe, but: **how a living person, using the life they have salvaged, can write a warm, credible, personally verifiable invitation to all fellow travelers still on the road.**
