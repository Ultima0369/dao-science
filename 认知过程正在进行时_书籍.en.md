## Preface: Seeking Truth in Misunderstanding

> "Heaven and earth have great beauty but do not speak; the four seasons have clear laws but do not argue; all things have their principles but do not explain."  
> — Zhuangzi, "Knowledge Wanders North"

This book was born from a simple observation: **human beings almost always live in misunderstanding.**

We misunderstand the world, others, and ourselves. This misunderstanding is not an accidental error but an inevitable product of the cognitive process. Just as light refracts into different colors when passing through a prism, information refracts into different understandings when passing through the human mind.

But misunderstanding is not terrible. What is terrible is **not knowing that we are misunderstanding.**

This book tries to answer three core questions:

1. **How does misunderstanding arise?** — from the perspective of cognitive science
2. **Why is misunderstanding inevitable?** — from the perspective of evolutionary psychology
3. **How can misunderstanding be reduced?** — from the perspective of practical wisdom

This is not a guidebook on "how to avoid misunderstanding." There are already too many such books, with limited effect. If misunderstanding is a cognitive inevitability, then "avoidance" itself is a kind of misunderstanding.

This is a book about **how to coexist with misunderstanding**: how, knowing that we will inevitably misunderstand, we can still understand, communicate, cooperate, and create.

Let us begin with the most fundamental question: **What exactly is the cognitive process?**
