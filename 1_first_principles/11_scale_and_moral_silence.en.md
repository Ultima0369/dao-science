# Scale and Moral Silence: Value Suspension Under Multi-Scale Maps

## Scale and Moral Silence: Value Suspension Under Multi-Scale Maps

---

## Abstract

This note begins with a street observation: cheap eggs sold at a loss, adulterated cooking oil, and psychological manipulation targeting the elderly. When the same event is zoomed out from the interpersonal scale to the ecological, planetary, and cosmological scales, the grain of moral judgment changes dramatically. We introduce the idea of **multi-scale maps**: different scales require different descriptive languages. At L7 (relational collapse), evil is a felt wound; at L2/L3 (pattern recognition), evil is an understandable system-dynamic deviation; at L0 (thing-in-itself / cosmological scale), human civilization is a brief fluctuation. True wisdom is not to cancel small-scale suffering with the largest possible scale, but to hold several maps at once while keeping an attentional bandwidth that can switch between them. The note closes with three first-person operational prompts to help the reader identify which map is currently in use and avoid mistaking one map for the whole territory.

**Keywords**: multi-scale maps; moral silence; scale zoom; value suspension; catfish effect; Universe 25; L0-L7 spectrum

## 1. From the street to the stars: the same event at different resolutions

You stand in the market and watch someone sell eggs at 4.5 yuan per jin while demanding that elderly customers add WeChat and join a group. In one minute you see the pattern: loss-leader bait → trust building → long-term harvest. You feel a sting, and you know you cannot save everyone.

Now zoom out:

- **Community scale**: This is fraud against a vulnerable population; it calls for reporting, warning, and community intervention.
- **City scale**: This is a mobile gang running high-frequency, low-sum, cross-district scams with enormous enforcement costs.
- **National / global scale**: This is a capillary lesion produced when monetary expansion suppresses returns on legitimate assets and capital seeks any "hole to fill."
- **Ecological / planetary scale**: Human civilization is a non-equilibrium steady state on Earth's surface; good and evil are internal entropy-flow labels.
- **Cosmological scale**: Earth, the Sun, and galaxies are transient structures. The whole of human history is a tiny fluctuation on the universe's clock.

The same event looks completely different at different scales. This is not relativism; it is a **difference in resolution**.

## 2. Moral silence: the right response when language runs out of grain

When the scale is stretched far enough, everyday moral vocabulary begins to fail. You wrote:

> "Objective to the point of coldness, yet not pretending to be calm like Heaven — it is the actual magnification of scale that leaves me speechless, silent."

This is **moral silence**: not indifference, not nihilism, but the receiver still on while the current linguistic channel cannot process the signal. Silence is the correct response when bandwidth is insufficient; it is not turning the receiver off.

### 2.1 Moral silence is not nihilism

Nihilism says: "Because the scale is vast, nothing matters." Moral silence says: "The vast scale leaves me temporarily speechless, but I still admit that the suffering at the small scale is real." The former cancels value; the latter suspends judgment.

### 2.2 Moral silence is not indifference

Indifference flees from the territory. Moral silence admits the boundary of one's vocabulary. You still ache for those elderly people; you simply no longer use interpersonal-scale language such as "evil will be rewarded" to explain planetary-scale phenomena.

## 3. Multi-scale maps: each map covers only one scale band

`03_map_not_territory.md` says: the map is not the territory. We add a further point: **the same territory needs multiple maps**. Each map is valid only within a specific scale band; using it across scales distorts judgment.

| Scale level | Suitable map | Keywords | Typical question |
|-------------|--------------|----------|------------------|
| L7 relational collapse | Ethics / law | harm, responsibility, repair | "Who is harmed? Who should be punished?" |
| L5/L6 emotion & narrative | Psychology / society | empathy, anger, collective memory | "Why do I feel this way?" |
| L2/L3 pattern recognition | Complexity science | feedback, attractors, deviation | "How does this pattern sustain itself?" |
| L0 thing-in-itself / cosmos | Thermodynamics / cosmology | entropy, fluctuation, steady state | "What is this on the universe's clock?" |

The crucial point: **one must not use the L0 map to answer an L7 question**. For example, canceling intervention against a present fraud because "the universe will eventually heat-death" is misusing a large-scale map as a small-scale action guide. Conversely, using L7's "just reward" to explain L0's galactic evolution is also a map misuse.

## 4. Two key metaphors: the catfish effect and Universe 25

### 4.1 The catfish effect

A catfish introduced into a school of fish creates disturbance and raises overall vitality. In complex systems, moderate deviation and conflict can prevent a system from dying of excessive stability. Evil at the local level may be destruction; at the system level it may be a form of "necessary noise" — provided it does not push the system past its tipping point.

### 4.2 Universe 25

John Calhoun's "Universe 25" experiment showed that when mice were given unlimited resources, no predators, and no challenges in a "utopia," social behavior gradually degraded and the population eventually collapsed. This experiment shatters the perfectionist illusion: **not only does "doing evil" carry a cost; the attempt to eliminate all deviation also carries a high cost**.

This forms a dual with `07_cost_of_deviation.md`: the cost of deviating from Dao is high, but the cost of trying to erase all deviation is equally high.

## 5. Operational prompts: how to know which map you are using

Three first-person, testable introspection prompts:

1. **Does my emotional intensity match the current scale?**  
   If a stellar explosion keeps you awake at night while a neighborhood fraud only makes you shrug, your scale-switching may be stuck.

2. **Am I using one map to cancel another?**  
   For example: "The universe is so big, what does a few hundred yuan fraud matter?" — using L0 to cancel L7. Or: "If we don't severely punish the scammer, we are enabling evil" — using L7 to cancel L2/L3 systems analysis.

3. **Is there a reachable meter in my action radius?**  
   No matter how large the scale, your local weight as a network node is non-zero. Warning one elderly person, filing one report, or writing one record are all real behavioral consequences.

## 6. Convergence: from silence back to a reachable meter

The silence produced by scale zoom is not the end point; it is a signal that your language needs upgrading, not that your action needs to stop.

You do not have to answer for the cracks in world M2, nor for all elderly people. You only have to answer for the part you can control: whether your awareness is still present at the next 4.5-yuan egg stall, at the next bucket of "cheap cooking oil."

This is the operational version of **"awakened interbeing" on a 21st-century street**: no lotus, no halo. Just someone who has seen the bucket of adulterated oil, still carrying a small ache for others, and not losing focus because of it.
