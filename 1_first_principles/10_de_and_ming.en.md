## 1. De: Attentional Allocation of Vital Energy

### 1.1 The Sole Criterion of De

In Eastern traditions, "De" (德) is often misread as moral prescription. But in the context of living practice, De has only one test:

> **From thought to action to effect feedback, does it maintain and improve the existence quality of individual A and individual B?**

Here:
- **Existence quality** is not an abstraction but an observable composite of bodily state, relational state, and group state.
- **Body and mind are cut in discussion, but inseparable in reality**: physical discomfort distorts mental judgment, and mental fixation produces physiological depletion.
- **Individual and group are nested**: a "De" that harms group existence quality will eventually harm the individual; one that maintains only the individual while destroying the group is also not true De.

Thus De is not some a priori moral law but a **verifiable effect of energy allocation**.

### 1.2 De as Attentional Energy Management

Operationally, De is equivalent to **a living system's attentional allocation of vital energy**:

- Knowing when to focus and when to relax
- Knowing when to act and when to wait
- Knowing which channel to invest limited resources in to produce positive feedback
- Knowing how to recover naturally after expenditure

De is therefore isomorphic to the project's "attention as precision optimization" (`2_models/attention_model.md`):

$$\text{De}(t) \equiv \text{attention allocation that minimizes expected free energy}$$

### 1.3 Mathematical Sketch of De

Suppose a living system at time $t$ has total energy budget $E(t)$ to allocate across channels or tasks $i$:

$$\sum_i a_i(t) = 1, \quad a_i(t) \geq 0$$

where $a_i(t)$ is the attention/energy fraction allocated to channel $i$. The quality of De can be assessed by the system's change in free energy:

$$\Delta F_{\text{De}} = \sum_i a_i(t) \cdot \left( \text{prediction error}_i + \lambda \cdot \text{cost}_i \right) - \text{recovery}_i$$

Good De keeps $\Delta F_{\text{De}}$ decreasing or at least non-worsening, manifesting as improved existence quality of individuals and groups. Bad De accumulates free energy, leading to dissipation, blockage, or collapse.

### 1.4 On Classifying and Rating De

For practical work, we can divide De into levels and metrics, e.g.:

- Elementary De: maintaining one's own basic existence quality
- Intermediate De: improving existence quality in relationships
- Advanced De: promoting emergent viability in larger groups

But these are **operational tools, not truth declarations**. The reality of De lies in one concrete question: does this allocation improve the existence quality of the relevant individuals and groups? Any rating system that departs from this test degenerates into a new veil.

### 1.5 De and the Node

In `1_first_principles/09_node_and_translation.md`, the node is defined as the translation layer between heterogeneous flows. De is the **energy quality** of a node:

- **Smooth De**: energy passes through the node without loss, lowering total free energy and improving existence quality.
- **Blocked De**: the node is monopolized by ego, energy is retained, local free energy rises, and existence quality worsens.
- **Restorability**: a good node naturally returns to transparency after dissipation.

Thus cultivating De can be understood as becoming an increasingly transparent node — not by eliminating self, but by letting energy smoothly serve the improvement of overall existence quality.

## 2. Ming: Penetrating Awareness That Unifies Yin and Yang

### 2.1 Ming Is the Immediate Capacity to Judge De

If De is energy allocation, then **Ming is the capacity to judge whether that allocation is good**. Whether De improves individual and group existence quality cannot wait for post-disaster review; it must be recognized immediately before, during, and after action. This immediate recognition is Ming.

Therefore De and Ming are not two separate things but two aspects of one process:

- **De** = attentional allocation of energy
- **Ming** = immediate insight into that allocation and its effects
- **De without Ming** = blind action, possibly producing harm with good intent
- **Ming without De** = clear seeing without executive power, insight wasted

### 2.2 Ming as Yin-Yang Integration

Ancient wisdom used "yin-yang" to metaphorize the operation of Ming:

- **Yin** = passive reception, containment, reflection (the mirror metaphor)
- **Yang** = active penetration, illumination, distinction (the light metaphor)
- **Ming** = integration of yin and yang: gathering information widely while focusing on what matters; acknowledging bandwidth limits while penetrating appearances to recognize patterns

This means Ming is not mere "seeing" but "knowing what to see and what to ignore after seeing."

### 2.3 The Information-Processing Structure of Ming

Ming can be described as information compression and pattern extraction under bandwidth constraints:

1. **Gather as much as possible**: within the attention budget, collect heterogeneous information (both yin and yang).
2. **Acknowledge overload**: recognize that full information cannot be processed simultaneously; key variables must be selected.
3. **Penetrate appearances**: identify structure, regularity, and invariant patterns amid noise.
4. **Store as constants**: encode identified regularities as memory, models, or priors.
5. **Fill in variables in real time**: when constants are reused, do not mechanically apply them; re-observe deeply and supplement with local, present variables.
6. **Output determinate action**: form executable decisions based on constants + variables.

In active-inference terms, Ming is:

$$\text{Ming}: \quad \underbrace{Q(s \mid o)}_{\text{real-time observation fills variables}} \times \underbrace{P(s)}_{\text{memory constants}} \to \underbrace{\pi}_{\text{determinate action}}$$

### 2.4 Ming as Active Yet Gentle Light

Ming is not only a passively reflecting mirror but also an actively penetrating light. Yet this light is not glaring:

- **Bright without dazzling**: illuminates things without blinding others
- **Harmonizing with dust**: merges with the environment without advertising its own clarity
- **The noble person harmonizes but does not conform**: maintains independent judgment within harmony, not blindly following

This is consistent with the project's relational attunement model (`2_models/relational_attunement.md`): Ming is both the receptive capacity to "be seen" and the active precision to "see the other."

### 2.5 Formalization of Ming: Hitting the Effective Range

Under limited information and limited energy, Ming continuously hits the **effective range** rather than pursuing **deterministic precision**:

$$\pi_{\text{ming}} \approx \arg\min_\pi G(\pi) \quad \text{s.t.} \quad \text{resources} \leq E_{\text{budget}}$$

The key is the "≈": Ming does not seek perfect optimality but a **good-enough, executable, correctable** solution. This is compatible with satisficing in reinforcement learning, optimal foraging theory in ecology, and expected free-energy minimization in active inference.

### 2.6 Evolutionary Validation of Ming

Ming is strictly tested by evolution:

- Effective decisions → survival → gene/culture continuation
- Ineffective decisions → depletion/predation/extinction → matter and energy re-enter the ecological cycle

This is falsifiability in the strongest sense: life forms that cannot adapt disappear quickly. Ming is therefore not metaphysics but **a necessary condition for life to persist**.

## 3. The Collaboration of De, Ming, and Dao

### 3.1 Least Action as Common Criterion

Dao, De, and Ming formally converge on the same criterion:

- **Dao**: $\pi_{\text{with Dao}} = \arg\min_\pi G(\pi)$
- **De**: allocate limited energy to the channels that most reduce $G$
- **Ming**: under bandwidth constraints, unify yin and yang, identify constants and variables, and approximately hit $\arg\min_\pi G(\pi)$

Together they form a closed loop:

```
Ming (gather + penetrate + recognize patterns)
  ↓
Decision (constants + real-time variables)
  ↓
De (energy allocation)
  ↓
Action
  ↓
Environmental feedback (improves or harms existence quality)
  ↓
Ming (update models of constants and variables)
```

### 3.2 Effective Range vs. Deterministic Precision

The characteristic of Ming is not accurate calculation but **hitting the effective range**. In real life:

- Waiting for perfect information misses the moment
- Pursuing absolute optimality exhausts energy
- Hitting the effective range first, then correcting in practice, is the survival strategy

This echoes the project's "reference frames prevent overload" (`1_first_principles/08_space_time_and_modeling.md`): reference frames, like Ming, are necessary simplifications to avoid information overload.

## 4. How Attentional Hijacking Veils Ming

Attentional hijacking is dangerous because it disrupts Ming's yin-yang integration:

- Excessive focus (yang dominates yin) → seeing only what one wants to see, ignoring context
- Excessive diffusion (yin dominates yang) → information scattered, unable to identify key variables
- Rigid memory constants → treating past regularities as present reality
- Missing real-time variables → blind to current signals

Healthy Ming requires dynamic balance between yin and yang, constants and variables, reception and penetration.

### 4.1 Physiological Hijacking

When the amygdala-prefrontal competition window (`2_models/100ms_model.md`) is hijacked by threat or reward signals:

- Large amounts of energy are forcibly allocated to a single channel
- Global awareness is compressed
- Ming is veiled, and decisions degenerate into instinctive reactions

### 4.2 Internal Drama Hijacking

When the default mode network (DMN) is overactive, the narrative self occupies awareness bandwidth (`2_models/dmn_self_model.md`):

- Energy is consumed in rumination, fantasy, and self-defense
- Precision in perceiving present reality drops
- Ming is veiled by story

### 4.3 One-Sided View Hijacking

Long immersion in a single thought system forms mental veils (`2_models/neuroplasticity_loop.md`):

- Precision is locked into existing priors
- New evidence is filtered as noise
- Ming is veiled by conceptual filters

### 4.4 Restoring Ming: Training De

Restoring Ming is not acquiring more knowledge but:

1. **Recognizing hijacking**: meta-awareness that attention is locked
2. **Reducing precision**: lowering the abnormally elevated channel precision
3. **Restarting awareness bandwidth**: letting DMN down-regulate and global awareness recover
4. **Reallocating energy**: directing De toward effective channels based on present reality

This is precisely what the project's methodology of Li-Ru and Xing-Ru aims to train.

## 5. Application Mapping

### 5.1 Individual Level

- De: managing energy, sleep, diet, exercise, attention training
- Ming: intuition, bodily wisdom, situational judgment
- Veiling: anxiety, depression, addiction, burnout

### 5.2 Relational Level

- De: appropriately releasing and holding attention in relationships, neither retaining nor overdrafting
- Ming: perceiving the other's real state rather than one's own projection
- Veiling: control, people-pleasing, coldness, rumination

### 5.3 Organizational Level

- De: directing organizational energy to key nodes, avoiding useless meetings and processes
- Ming: recognizing true signals in market, team, and technology
- Veiling: bureaucracy, information cocoons, ego politics

### 5.4 Carbon-Silicon Level

- De: reasonably allocating energy and tasks between humans and AI
- Ming: recognizing which decisions must be made by humans and which can be delegated to AI
- Veiling: over-reliance on AI or excessive fear of AI

## 6. Falsification Conditions

Evidence that would weaken this principle:

1. Discovery that living systems can persist long-term with completely random or non-optimized energy allocation.
2. Proof that some energy allocation improves individual A's existence quality while necessarily harming individual B's, with no reallocation that achieves mutual benefit.
3. Proof that attentional hijacking does not degrade decision quality or survival outcomes.
4. Showing that the feeling of "Ming" is unrelated to actual adaptiveness.
5. Proof that the principle of least action does not apply to behavioral choice in living systems.
6. Discovery that after rating De in hierarchical levels, the rating itself becomes a new veil over the existence-quality test.

## 7. Current Limitations

- The neural correlates of De and Ming are not yet precisely located.
- Experimental paradigms centered on "improvement in existence quality" still need to be built.
- The "effective range" concept of Ming needs sharper operationalization.
- Connection to concrete first-person training data (e.g., N-of-1) is still pending.
- Ratings and classifications of De are easily mistaken for truth and require ongoing methodological vigilance.
