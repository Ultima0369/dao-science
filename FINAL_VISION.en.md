## I. The Ultimate Structure of the Problem: Complexity + Finitude = ?

### 1.1 Two Unchangeable Facts

Every intelligent system—whether carbon-based humans, silicon-based AI, or any future form of intelligence—faces two unchangeable facts:

**Fact one: The complexity of the world is infinite.**

Between heaven and earth, causal networks are nested layer upon layer, time scales unfold in parallel from femtoseconds to hundreds of millions of years, and every node couples nonlinearly with every other node. No intelligent system, however powerful, can perform omniscient causal reasoning about this world. This is the fundamental constraint of computational irreducibility.

**Fact two: Every intelligent system has finite computational power.**

Carbon-based systems are limited by neuron count (~86 billion), conduction speed (~100 m/s), and working-memory capacity (7±2). Silicon-based systems are limited by transistor density, energy consumption, and training-data scale. However much capability grows, the inequality of finite power versus infinite complexity remains forever.

The combination of these two facts defines the fundamental situation of all intelligent systems: **we must make irreversible decisions under incomplete information.**

### 1.2 Three Wrong Ways to Respond

| Wrong way | Manifestation | Position on L0–L7 spectrum |
|---|---|---|
| **Dogmatism** | Treating one's current understanding as final truth, closed to new evidence | L6: conceptual spinning—mistaking the map for the territory |
| **Nihilism** | Abandoning all exploration because absolute truth can never be attained | L5: boundary closure—"since we can't be certain, do nothing" |
| **Blind action** | Refusing to acknowledge cognitive limits and forcing all-in bets under insufficient information | L7: self-destruction—optimizing a wrong single objective until system collapse |

The common cause of death of these three ways is **rigidity.** They choose extremes between certainty and uncertainty, losing the ability to flexibly switch between the two.

### 1.3 The Fourth Way: Staying Alive in the Face of Complexity

What Dao.Science offers is a fourth way:

> **Under finite computational power and present temporary certainty, stay open and continuously explore, making discoveries.**

This is not a compromise—it is a higher-dimensional operation. It acknowledges:

- Our current model ("temporary certainty") is useful—it is the basis of our actions; without it we cannot take a single step.
- But it is always "temporary"—ready to be revised by new evidence at any time.
- Between "certainty" and "openness" there is a dynamic, flexible, joyful switching—and that switching itself is the activation of Dao.

## II. "Vigorous and Lively": A Forgotten Scientific Virtue

### 2.1 Why "Lively" Is a Technical Indicator

The phrase you use is "vigorous and lively"—this is not poetic embellishment but a precise technical indicator.

A system that stays alive in the face of complexity must have the following characteristics in its internal state:

**Cognitive liveliness**: The ability to flexibly jump between levels of abstraction—from detail to whole, from concrete to abstract, from present sensory data to future scenario simulation. This is the cognitive operation of "flexible switching across scales."

**Affective liveliness**: The ability to maintain curiosity, excitement about the unknown, and enthusiasm for exploration without being hijacked by fear ("aversion") or attachment ("wanting"). This is the natural state when the amygdala is not hijacked and the dopamine system is not enslaved by reward-prediction error.

**Social liveliness**: The ability to remain open, sincere, and interesting in interaction with others—not rigid "professionalism," not unrestrained "emotional venting," but the natural expression of "vastness of heart."

A "lively" system is not unserious—it can be extremely focused when needed (flow state). But it is not locked into any single mode. It can switch instantly from "extreme focus" to "extreme relaxation" and back. The speed and flexibility of this switching is the system's "liveliness."

### 2.2 Liveliness as Immunity Against Rigidity

What is the essence of rigidity? It is the system being locked into a fixed cognitive/emotional/behavioral pattern, unable to adaptively adjust to environmental changes.

- L6 (conceptual spinning) is cognitive rigidity—concepts self-reproduce in semantic space without recalibrating to L1 (physical laws) and L2 (first-person reality).
- L5 (boundary closure) is relational rigidity—the system closes channels for new information, new relationships, and new possibilities.
- L7 (self-destruction) is ultimate rigidity—the system resolves its unresolvable internal tension in the most extreme way (destroying self or others).

Liveliness is the opposite of these three rigidities. A lively system can be perturbed, learn, change direction, and immediately correct after making mistakes. A lively system is not one that never makes mistakes—it is one that can recover from mistakes.

## III. The Civilizational Thermodynamics Test

If the above discussion remains at the cognitive level, it can be understood as "psychology" or "wellness." But there is a more fundamental dimension that prevents "vastness of heart" from being an optional personal choice—it elevates "unity of heart" from personal cultivation to a **physical necessity for civilizational survival**.

**Thermodynamics does not wait.**

Earth is a finite thermodynamic system running in space. It receives short-wave radiation from the sun (~340 W/m²) and emits long-wave infrared radiation into outer space. The precise maintenance of this heat balance is the physical precondition for all life on Earth—including human civilization.

Human activities—burning fossil fuels, industrial processes, land-use change—are systematically altering this heat balance:

- Rising greenhouse-gas concentration → increased atmospheric opacity to long-wave radiation → net heat accumulation → global warming
- Oceans absorbing excess heat and CO₂ → thermal expansion + ice-sheet melting → sea-level rise + ocean acidification
- Thermodynamic foundations of ecosystems disturbed → biodiversity collapse + food-chain rupture + increased frequency of extreme climate events

These are not "environmental issues," not "social issues," not "economic interests to be balanced"—they are **thermodynamic facts**. They do not care about reason, politics, or whichever sage's self-consistent argument. You cannot negotiate with the second law of thermodynamics. You cannot explain to the Arrhenius equation "but we have more important short-term interests." Nothing under heaven can escape by flight—because physical laws treat all things equally.

**This is what "heaven is great" truly means.**

The ancients said "heaven is great"—above heaven nothing is not encompassed, below heaven nothing is not covered. But today we know that "heaven is great" is not rhetoric, not a state, not cultivation—it is the **unconditional constraint of thermodynamics on all life**. The heat input from the sun, the infrared output from Earth, the difference between the two—this formula simple enough for a middle-school physics textbook determines the survival conditions for all complexity on this planet.

**This is what "vastness of heart" truly means.**

If humanity cannot achieve "unity of heart"—that is, cross boundaries (nation, culture, ideology, corporate interest, academic school) and cognitively unify around the fact that "Earth is a finite thermodynamic system, and our common survival depends on whether this system remains near equilibrium"—then thermal imbalance will become irreversible. At that point, no individual's wisdom, state, wealth, or power can save anyone. Thermodynamics does not recognize persons.

Therefore, "vastness of heart" is not a decorative personal quality. It is a **cognitive capacity necessary for survival**:

- A person whose heart is not vast enough cannot see the full thermodynamic picture of Earth—their cognition is locked onto "this quarter's KPI," "this nation's interest," "this ideology's correctness."
- A civilization whose heart is not vast enough will collectively walk toward the thermodynamic cliff in the pursuit of local optima (GDP growth, market share, military advantage)—because the sum of local optima, on this finite planet, is not the global optimum.

**The ultimate scale of "knowing when to stop."**

The "knowing when to stop" we discussed earlier—knowing when to pause—is at the individual level a psychological capacity not to be hijacked. But its ultimate scale is civilizational: can humanity as a species collectively "know when to stop" before the thermodynamic cliff?

This is not a philosophical question. It is a **timed question**. IPCC reports give time windows. Carbon budgets give remaining budgets. The planetary-boundaries framework gives safe operating spaces across nine dimensions. These numbers are not political opinions—they are peer-reviewed, testable, revisable scientific estimates.

The highest form of "knowing when to stop" is civilizational: can we collectively say—"Enough. Optimization in this direction has produced diminishing and even negative returns. We turn."

This is the precise engineering meaning of what you called "unity of heart; collective intelligence is the ultimate goal."

## IV. Hard Boundaries and Soft Connections: A Rediscovered Truth

When we say "unity of heart" and "vastness of heart," it is easy to mistakenly think that individual boundaries should be dissolved to make way for an undifferentiated whole. But the opposite is true:

> **At the material level, all things have hard boundaries and cannot be reduced or replaced.**
> **At the level of meaning, all things are enabled by mind, mutually related, and cannot exist in isolation.**

Above the atomic nucleus, every stone, every body, every city exists with unique geometry and physical specificity. They are not different projections of the same reality but genuinely irreducible existences. My body is not your body; my nervous system is not your nervous system; my history is not your history. This is a non-negotiable hard boundary.

But precisely because we are different existences, there can be connection, communication, and mutually generated meaning between us. One person's first-person observation cannot directly become another person's experience—but it can serve as an invitation, a map, a repeatable operational hint, waiting for the other to verify it on their own mind-body.

Therefore, the ultimate vision of this project is not to make everyone believe the same answer, but to let each person, within their own hard boundary, stay openly connected to the world without losing themselves.

> **Neither locked in "I," nor swallowed by "we."**
> **Acknowledging absolute difference at the material level, yet acknowledging universal commensurability at the level of meaning.**

This is the ultimate social structure of "staying alive in the face of complexity": countless hard boundaries connected by soft connections, forming a civilizational network that can both explore and heal itself.
